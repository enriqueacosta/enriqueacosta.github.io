Solution 2.1.1.

The continuous percent growth rate is the value of \(k\) in the equation \(Q=ae^{kt}\text{,}\) which is \(-\text{.}\)

To convert to the form \(Q=ab^t\text{,}\) we first say that the right sides of the two equations equal each other (since each equals \(Q\)), and then we solve for \(a\) and \(b\text{.}\) Thus, we have \(ab^{t}=e^{- t}\text{.}\) At \(t=0\text{,}\) we can solve for \(a\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} ab^{0} \amp = e^{- \cdot 0} \\ a\cdot 1 \amp = 1 \\ a \amp = 1. \end{align*}

Thus, we have \(1b^{t}=e^{- t}\text{,}\) and we solve for \(b\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} 1b^t \amp = e^{- t} \\ b^{t} \amp = e^{- t} \\ b^t \amp = \left(e^{- }\right)^t \\ b \amp = e^{- }\approx. \end{align*}

Therefore, the equation is \(Q=1()^{t}\text{.}\)
