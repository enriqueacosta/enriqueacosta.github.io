Solution 13.1.1.

Let \(Q(t) \) be the mass of the substance at time \(t \text{,}\) and \(Q_0 \) be the initial mass of the substance. Since the substance is decaying at a continuous rate, we know that \(Q(t)=Q_0e^{kt} \) where \(k=- \) (The decay is \(\% \)). So \(Q(t)=Q_0e^{- t} \text{.}\) We want to know when \(Q(t)=\frac{1}{2}Q_0 \text{.}\)

\begin{align*} Q_0 e^{- t} \amp = \frac{1}{2}Q_0 \\ e^{- t} \amp = \frac{1}{2} \\ \ln e^{- t} \amp = \ln\left(\frac{1}{2}\right) \\ - t \amp = \ln\left(\frac{1}{2}\right) \\ t \amp = \frac{\ln\frac{1}{2}}{- } \approx \end{align*}

So the half-life is about \(\) minutes.
