Item 18.1.b.

If the interest is compounded only once a year, the formula we will use is \(P(t)=P_0b^t\) where \(b\) is the percent value of what the investment had been one year earlier. If it is earning \({7}\%\) interest compounded once a year, it is \(10{7}\%\) of what it had been the previous year, so our formula is \(P(t)=P_{0}({1.07})^{t}.\)

Using this new formula, we will now solve

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} P(t) \amp = {6} P_0 \\ P_0({1.07})^{t} \amp = {6} P_{0} \\ ({1.07})^{t} \amp = {6} \\ \log ({1.07})^t \amp = \log {6} \\ t \log {1.07} \amp = \log {6} \\ t \amp = \frac{\log {6} }{\log {1.07}}\approx {26.482} \hbox{ years.} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

So, compounding once a year, it will take about \(26.5\) years for the investment to sextuple.
