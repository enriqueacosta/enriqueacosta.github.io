Solution 4.7.1

To find the zeros, we solve the equation

\begin{equation*} 0={94} x^2+55x+{39} \text{.} \end{equation*}

This does not appear to be factorable. Thus, we use the quadratic formula with \(a={94} \text{,}\) \(b=55\text{,}\) and \(c={39} \text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} x \amp =\amp \frac{-55\pm\sqrt{55^2-4({94} )({39} )}}{2({94} )}\\ x \amp =\amp \frac{-55\pm\sqrt{-{11639} }}{178} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Since \(\sqrt{-{11639} }\) is undefined, there are no zeros.
