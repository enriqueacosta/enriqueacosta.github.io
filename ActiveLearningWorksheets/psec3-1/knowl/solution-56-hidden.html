We know there are zeros at \(x=-6\) and \(x=2\text{,}\) so we use the factored form:

\begin{equation*} y=a(x+6)(x-2) \end{equation*}

and solve for \(a\text{.}\) At \(x=0\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} 5 \amp=\amp a(0+6)(0-2) \\ 5 \amp=\amp -12 a \\ -\frac{5}{12} \amp=\amp a. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Thus,

\begin{equation*} y=-\frac{5}{12}(x+6)(x-2) \end{equation*}

or

\begin{equation*} y = -\frac{5}{12} x^2-\frac{5}{3}x +5. \end{equation*}
