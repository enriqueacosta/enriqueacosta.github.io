We know there are zeros at \(x=-\) and \(x=\text{,}\) so we use the factored form:

\begin{equation*} y=a(x+)(x-) \end{equation*}

and solve for \(a\text{.}\) At \(x=0\text{,}\) we have

\begin{align*} \amp=\amp a(0+)(0-) \\ \amp=\amp - a \\ -\frac{}{} \amp=\amp a. \end{align*}

Thus,

\begin{equation*} y=-\frac{}{}(x+)(x-) \end{equation*}

or

\begin{equation*} y = -\frac{}{} x^2-\frac{}{}x +. \end{equation*}
