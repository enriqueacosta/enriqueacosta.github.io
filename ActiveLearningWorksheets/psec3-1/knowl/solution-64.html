Solution 6.22.1

Since the parabola has \(x\)-intercepts at \(x=-5\) and \(x=9 \text{,}\) its formula is:

\begin{equation*} y=a(x+5 )(x-9 ). \end{equation*}

The coordinates \((-8, 4)\) must satisfy the equation, so

\begin{equation*} 4=a(-8+5 )(-8-9 ). \end{equation*}

Solving for \(a\) gives \(a=\frac{4}{51}\text{.}\) The formula is:

\begin{equation*} y=\frac{4}{51} (x+5 )(x-9 ). \end{equation*}
