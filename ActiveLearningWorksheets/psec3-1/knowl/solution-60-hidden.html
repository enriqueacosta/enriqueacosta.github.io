There is one zero at \(x=-{4}\text{,}\) so by symmetry the vertex is \((-{4},0)\text{.}\) We have \(y=a(x+{4})^2\text{.}\) Solving for \(a\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} a(0+{4})^2\amp =\amp {11} \\ {16} a\amp =\amp {11} \\ a \amp =\amp \frac{{11} }{{16}}, \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

so \(y={\frac{11}{16}\!\left(x+4\right)^{2}}\text{.}\)
