Solution 4.8.1

We solve for \(r\) in the equation \(Q(r)={4} r^2-{8} r-{192}=0\) using the quadratic formula with \(a={4}\text{,}\) \(b=-{8}\) and \(c=-{192}\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} r\amp =\amp \frac{-(-{8}) \pm \sqrt{(-{8})^2-4({4})(-{192})}}{2({4})}\\ r\amp =\amp \frac{{8} \pm \sqrt{{64}+{3072}}}{{8}}\\ r\amp =\amp \frac{{8} \pm \sqrt{{3136}}}{{8}}\\ r\amp =\amp \frac{{8} \pm {56}}{{8}} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Therefore \(r=({8} + {56})/{8}={8}\) and \(r=({8} - {56})/{8}=-{6}\text{.}\) The zeros of \(Q(r)\) are \(r={8}\) and \(r=-{6}\text{.}\)
