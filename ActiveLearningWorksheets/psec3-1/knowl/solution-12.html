Solution 4.6.1

To find the zeros, we solve the equation

\begin{equation*} 0 = {49} x^2+{14} x+1. \end{equation*}

We see that this is factorable, as follows:

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} y \amp =\amp ({7} x+1)({7} x+1)\\ y \amp =\amp ({7} x+1)^2. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Therefore, there is only one zero at \(x=-\frac{1}{{7}}\text{.}\)
