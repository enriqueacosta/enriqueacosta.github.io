We know there are zeros at \(x=-1\) and \(x=3\text{,}\) so we use the factored form:

\begin{equation*} y=a(x+1)(x-3) \end{equation*}

and solve for \(a\text{.}\) At \(x=0\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} 1 \amp=\amp a(0+1)(0-3) \\ 1 \amp=\amp -3 a \\ -\frac{1}{3} \amp=\amp a. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Thus,

\begin{equation*} y=-\frac{1}{3}(x+1)(x-3) \end{equation*}

or

\begin{equation*} y = -\frac{1}{3} x^2-\frac{2}{3}x +1. \end{equation*}
