Solution 5.8.1

We solve for \(r\) in the equation \(Q(r)={2} r^2-{8} r-{64}=0\) using the quadratic formula with \(a={2}\text{,}\) \(b=-{8}\) and \(c=-{64}\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} r\amp =\amp \frac{-(-{8}) \pm \sqrt{(-{8})^2-4({2})(-{64})}}{2({2})}\\ r\amp =\amp \frac{{8} \pm \sqrt{{64}+{512}}}{{4}}\\ r\amp =\amp \frac{{8} \pm \sqrt{{576}}}{{4}}\\ r\amp =\amp \frac{{8} \pm {24}}{{4}} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Therefore \(r=({8} + {24})/{4}={8}\) and \(r=({8} - {24})/{4}=-{4}\text{.}\) The zeros of \(Q(r)\) are \(r={8}\) and \(r=-{4}\text{.}\)
