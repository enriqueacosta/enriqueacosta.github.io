There is one zero at \(x=-{7}\text{,}\) so by symmetry the vertex is \((-{7},0)\text{.}\) We have \(y=a(x+{7})^2\text{.}\) Solving for \(a\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} a(0+{7})^2\amp =\amp {16} \\ {49} a\amp =\amp {16} \\ a \amp =\amp \frac{{16} }{{49}}, \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

so \(y={\frac{16}{49}\!\left(x+7\right)^{2}}\text{.}\)
