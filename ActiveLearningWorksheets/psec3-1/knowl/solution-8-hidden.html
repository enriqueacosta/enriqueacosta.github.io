We solve for \(r\) in the equation \(Q(r)={3} r^2-{3} r-{60}=0\) using the quadratic formula with \(a={3}\text{,}\) \(b=-{3}\) and \(c=-{60}\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} r\amp =\amp \frac{-(-{3}) \pm \sqrt{(-{3})^2-4({3})(-{60})}}{2({3})}\\ r\amp =\amp \frac{{3} \pm \sqrt{{9}+{720}}}{{6}}\\ r\amp =\amp \frac{{3} \pm \sqrt{{729}}}{{6}}\\ r\amp =\amp \frac{{3} \pm {27}}{{6}} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Therefore \(r=({3} + {27})/{6}={5}\) and \(r=({3} - {27})/{6}=-{4}\text{.}\) The zeros of \(Q(r)\) are \(r={5}\) and \(r=-{4}\text{.}\)
