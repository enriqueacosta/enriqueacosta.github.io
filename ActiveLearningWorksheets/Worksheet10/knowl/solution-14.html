Solution 1.2.7.1.

When we differentiate with respect to \(x\text{,}\) we think of \(y\) as a constant. Thus,

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} f_x(x,y)\amp=\amp \frac{\partial}{\partial x}\left(x^2y-2x^2+3xy^2\right) \\ \amp=\amp \frac{\partial}{\partial x}\left(x^2y\right)-\frac{\partial}{\partial x}\left(2x^2\right)+\frac{\partial}{\partial x}\left(3xy^2\right)\\ \amp =\amp 2xy - 4x + 3y^2 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
