A critical point of a function \(f(x,y)\) is a point where \(\nabla f(x,y)=\vec{0}\) or where \(\nabla f(x,y)\) is undefined. The partial derivatives

\begin{align*} f_x(x,y)\amp = -2-2x\\ f_y(x,y)\amp = 4-8y, \end{align*}

show that \(\nabla f(x,y)\) is defined everywhere, and \(\nabla f(x,y)=\vec{0}\) only when \((x,y)=(-1,1/2)\text{.}\) Thus, the only critical point of \(f\) is \((-1,1/2)\text{.}\)
