Solution 5.2.1.

A critical point of a function \(g(x,y)\) is a point where \(\nabla g(x,y)=\vec{0}\) or where \(\nabla g(x,y)\) is undefined. The partial derivatives

\begin{align*} g_x(x,y)\amp = 10x+4y+4 \\ g_y(x,y)\amp = 4x-4y-4, \end{align*}

show that \(\nabla g(x,y)\) is defined everywhere, and \(\nabla g(x,y)=\vec{0}\) only at the points which are solutions the system of equations

\begin{align*} 10x+4y+4 \amp = 0 \\ 4x-4y-4 \amp = 0. \end{align*}

If we add these two equations together, the \(4y\) will cancel giving \(14x=0\text{,}\) so \(x=0\text{.}\) Setting \(x=0\) in either of the two equations gives \(y=-1\text{.}\) Thus, the only solution to the system is \((x,y)=(0,-1)\text{.}\) Thus, the only critical point of \(g\) is \((0,-1)\text{.}\)

We compute \(D\)

\begin{equation*} D=\det \pmatrix{g_{xx} \amp g_{xy} \\ g_{yx} \amp g_{yy}}=\det \pmatrix{10 \amp 4 \\ 4 \amp -4}=-56, \end{equation*}

and since \(D\lt 0\) (at any point so in particular at \((0,-1)\)), we see that \((0,-1)\) is a saddle point.
