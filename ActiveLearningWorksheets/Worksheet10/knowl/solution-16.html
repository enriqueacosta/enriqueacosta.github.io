Solution 1.2.8.1.

When we differentiate with respect to \(y\text{,}\) we think of \(x\) as a constant. Thus,

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} g_y(x,y)\amp =\amp \frac{\partial}{\partial y}\left(3y-5x^2y+6xy^2+4\right)\\ \amp =\amp \frac{\partial}{\partial y}\left(3y\right)-\frac{\partial}{\partial y}\left(5x^2y\right)+\frac{\partial}{\partial y} \left(6xy^2\right)+\frac{\partial}{\partial y}\left(4\right)\\ \amp =\amp 3-5x^2+12xy \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
