Factoring out a \(2y\) in the second equation gives \(2y(1+x)=0\text{,}\) so either \(2y=0\) or \(1+x=0\) (or both). This shows that any solution \((x,y)\) to the system must have \(y=0\) or \(x=-1\text{.}\) Setting \(y=0\) in the first equation gives \[10x+6x^2=0,\] which has solutions \(x=0\) or \(x=-5/3\text{.}\) Thus, \((x,y)=(0,0)\) and \((x,y)=(-5/3,0)\) are two solutions (the only solutions with \(y=0\)). Setting \(x=-1\) in the first equation gives \(-4+y^2=0\text{,}\) so \(y=\pm 2\text{.}\) Thus, \((x,y)=(-1,-2)\) and \((x,y)=(-1,2)\) are two more solutions (the only solutions with \(x=-1\)).

The solutions to the system are \((0,0)\text{,}\) \((-5/3,0)\text{,}\) \((-1,-2)\) and \((-1,2)\text{.}\)
