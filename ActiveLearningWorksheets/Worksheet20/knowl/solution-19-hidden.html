First, \(S\) can be parameterized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(u,v)=\cos{(u)} \vec{i} +v \vec{j} +\sin{(u)}\vec{k} \end{equation*}

where \(0\leq v\leq 3\) and \(0\leq u\leq \pi\text{.}\)

The normal vector is \(\vec{N} =\cos{(u)} \vec{i} +\sin{(v)}\vec{k}\) and so \(||N||=1\text{.}\) Thus we have

\begin{align*} \int \int_{S} z\,dS \amp =\int_{0}^{3} \int_{0}^{\pi} \sin{(u)} \,du\,dv\\ \amp =3 \left( -\cos{(u)} \right)\large|_{0}^{\pi} \\ \amp =3 \left( -\cos{(\pi)} -(-\cos{(0)}) \right)\\ \amp =3\cdot (1-(-1))\\ \amp =6. \end{align*}
