First, \(S\) can be parameterized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(u,v)=u \vec{i} +v \vec{j} +(u^2+v^2)\vec{k}. \end{equation*}

Since we want \(z\leq 4\text{,}\) the region \(D\) mut be the disc \(z=u^2+v^2\leq 4\text{.}\) The normal vector is \(\vec{N} =-2u \vec{i} -2v \vec{j} +\vec{k}\) and so \(||N||=\sqrt{1+4u^2+4v^2}\text{.}\) Therefore, we have

\begin{equation*} \int_{S} 2dS =\int \int_D 2\sqrt{1+4u^2+4v^2}dudv. \end{equation*}

Since we are integrating over a disc, we use a polar change of coordinates with bounds \(0\leq r\leq 2\) and \(0\leq \theta \leq 2\pi\) giving:

\begin{align*} \int_{S} 2\,dS \amp =\int \int_D 2\sqrt{1+4u^2+4v^2}\,du\,dv\\ \amp =2 \int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{2} \sqrt{\left(1+4r^2\cos^2{(\theta )}+4r^2\sin^2{(\theta )}\right)} \,rdr\,d\theta\\ \amp =2 \int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{2} \left(1+4r^2\right)^{\frac{1}{2}} \,rdr\,d\theta\\ \amp =2 \cdot 2\pi \left(1+4r^2\right)^{\frac{3}{2}} \cdot \frac{2}{3} \cdot \frac{1}{8} \bigg|_{r=0}^{r=2}\\ \amp =\frac{\pi }{3}\left( 17^{\frac{3}{2}} -1\right). \end{align*}
