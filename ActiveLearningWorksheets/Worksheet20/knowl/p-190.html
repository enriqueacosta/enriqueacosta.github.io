The normal vector is \(\vec{N} =\sqrt{36-v^2} \cos{(u)} \vec{i} +\sqrt{36-v^2} \sin{(u)} \vec{j} +v\vec{k}\) and so \(||N||=36\text{.}\) Thus we have

\begin{align*} \int_{S} (2x^2+2y^2+2z^2)dS \amp =\int_{0}^{6} \int_{0}^{2\pi} \left[ 2\left(\sqrt{36-v^2} \cos{(u)}\right)^2\right.\\ \amp\qquad \left.+2\left(\sqrt{36-v^2} \sin{(u)}\right)^2 +2v^2\right](6)\,du\,dv\\ \amp =12 \int_{0}^{6} \int_{0}^{2\pi} \left[ (36-v^2) \cos^2{(u)}\right.\\ \amp\qquad\left.+(36-v^2) \sin^2{(u)}+v^2\right]\,du\,dv\\ \amp =12 \int_{0}^{6} \int_{0}^{2\pi} \left( 36-v^2+v^2\right)\,du\,dv\\ \amp =12 \int_{0}^{6} \int_{0}^{2\pi} 36\,du\,dv\\ \amp =12\cdot 6\cdot 2\pi \cdot 36\\ \amp = 5184\pi. \end{align*}
