First, \(S\) can be parameterized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(u,v)=3\cos{(u)} \vec{i} +3\sin{(u)} \vec{j} +v\vec{k} \end{equation*}

where \(0\leq v\leq 3\) and \(-\pi /2\leq u\leq \pi /2\text{.}\)

The normal vector is \(\vec{N} =3\cos{(u)} \vec{i} +3\sin{(u)} \vec{j}\) and so \(||N||=3\text{.}\) Thus we have

\begin{align*} \int_{S} y \,dS \amp =\int_{0}^{3} \int_{-\pi /2}^{\pi /2} 3\sin{(u)} \cdot 3\,du\,dv\\ \amp =9 \int_{0}^{3} \int_{-\pi /2}^{\pi /2} \sin{(u)} \,du\,dv\\ \amp =9\cdot 3 \cos{(u)}\large|_{-\pi /2}^{\pi /2}\\ \amp =0. \end{align*}
