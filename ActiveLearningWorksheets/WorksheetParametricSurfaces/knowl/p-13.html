Since they appear frequently, it is useful to be familiar with the formulas for normal vectors in the following cases:

  • Normal vectors to graphs. If a surface \(S\) is the part of the graph of a function \(z=f(x,y)\) and parameterized by \(\vec{r}(u,v) =u\vec{i} +v\vec{j} +f(u,v)\vec{k}\text{,}\) then
    \begin{equation*} \vec{N} =-f_u\vec{i} -f_v\vec{j} +\vec{k}\text{.} \end{equation*}
  • Normal vectors to surfaces generated by circles. If a surface \(S\) has contours which consist of concentric circles centered on the \(z\)-axis and is parameterized by \(\vec{r}(u,v) =f(v) \cos{(i)} \vec{i} +f(v)\sin{(u)} \vec{j} +v\vec{k}\) then
    \begin{equation*} \vec{N} =f(v) \cos{(u)} \vec{i} +f(v)\sin{(u)} \vec{j} +f(v)f'(v)\vec{k}\text{.} \end{equation*}
