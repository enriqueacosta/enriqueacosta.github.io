Since \(x=u\cos(v)\text{,}\) \(y=u\sin(v)\) and \(z=\sqrt{1-u^2}\text{,}\) the contours of the surface \(S\) will be concentric circles about the \(z\)-axis whose radius decreases with height \(z\) converging to a point when \(z=1\text{.}\) Thus \(S\) is the top half of the sphere \(x^2+y^2+z^2=1\) with \(z\geq 0\text{.}\) This can also be verified algebraically as substituting \(x=u\cos(v)\text{,}\) \(y=u\sin(v)\) into the equation \(x^2+y^2+z^2=1\) and solving for \(z\) gives \(z=\sqrt{1-u^2}\text{.}\)
