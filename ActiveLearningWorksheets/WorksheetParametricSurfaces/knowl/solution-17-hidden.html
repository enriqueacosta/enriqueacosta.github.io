Since \(x=\cos(v)\text{,}\) \(y=\sin(v)\) and \(z=u\text{,}\) the contours of the surface \(S\) will be concentric circles about the \(z\)-axis whose radius is always equal to \(1\text{.}\) Thus \(S\) is the graph of the cylinder \(x^2+y^2=1\text{.}\) This can also be verified algebraically as \(x=\cos(v)\text{,}\) \(y=\sin(v)\) satisfy the equation \(1=x^2+y^2\text{.}\)
