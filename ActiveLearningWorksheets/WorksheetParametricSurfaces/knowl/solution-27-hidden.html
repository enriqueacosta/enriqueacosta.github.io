The sphere \(x^2+y^2+z^2=9\) has contours which consist of concentric circles centered on the \(z\)-axis whose radii change at different heights. Specifically, for a given \(z\) since we can rewrite \(x^2+y^2+z^2=9\) as \(x^2+y^2=(9-z^2)=r^2\text{,}\) the radius \(r\) of the contour at height \(z\) will be \(r=\sqrt{9-z^2}\text{.}\) Therefore, the sphere can be parameterized by letting \(z=v\text{,}\) \(x=\sqrt{9-v^2}\cos{(u)}\) and \(y=\sqrt{9-v^2}\sin{(u)}\text{,}\) or

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(u,v) =\sqrt{9-v^2}\cos{(u)} \vec{i} +\sqrt{9-v^2}\sin{(u)} \vec{j} +v\vec{k}\text{.} \end{equation*}
