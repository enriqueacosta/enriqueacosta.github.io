Since \(x=u\text{,}\) \(y=v\) and \(z=2-u-v\text{,}\) in order to be in the first quadrant, we must have all three inequalities \(u\geq 0\text{,}\) \(v\geq 0\) and \(2-u-v\geq 0\) true. This last inequality gives \(v\leq 2-u\text{.}\) Sketching the region \(u\geq 0\text{,}\) \(v\geq 0\) and \(v\leq 2-u\text{,}\) we get a triangle with corresponding bounds \(0\leq u\leq 2\) and \(0\leq v\leq 2-u\text{,}\) or equivalently \(0\leq v\leq 2\) and \(0\leq u\leq 2-v\)
