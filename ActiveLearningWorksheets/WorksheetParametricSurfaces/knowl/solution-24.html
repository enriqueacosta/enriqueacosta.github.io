Solution 6.3.1.

The vectors \(\vec{PQ}=-3\vec{i} -\vec{j} -3\vec{k}\) and \(\vec{PR} =\vec{i} -\vec{j} -\vec{k}\) are both in the plane. Therefore, letting \(\vec{P} =\vec{i} +2\vec{j} +3\vec{k}\text{,}\) a parameterization for the plane will be

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \vec{r}(u,v) \amp =\vec{P} +u\vec{PQ} +v\vec{PR} \\ \amp =(1-3u+v)\vec{i} +(2-u-v)\vec{j} +(3-3u-v)\vec{k}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
