Since \(x=u\cos(v)\text{,}\) \(y=u\sin(v)\) and \(z=u^2\text{,}\) the contours of the surface \(S\) will be concentric circles about the \(z\)-axis whose radius \(u=\sqrt{z}\) is the square root of the height -- that is, the radius increases with height but at a slower and slower rate. Thus \(S\) is the graph of the paraboloid \(z=x^2+y^2\text{.}\) This can also be verified algebraically as substituting \(x=u\cos(v)\text{,}\) \(y=u\sin(v)\) into the equation \(z=x^2+y^2\) gives \(z=u^2\text{.}\)
