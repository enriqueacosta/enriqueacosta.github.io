1. Since \(x=u\text{,}\) \(y=v\) and \(z=2-u-v\text{,}\) in order to be in the first quadrant, we must have all three inequalities \(u\geq 0\text{,}\) \(v\geq 0\) and \(2-u-v\geq 0\) true. This last inequality gives \(v\leq 2-u\text{.}\) Sketching the region \(u\geq 0\text{,}\) \(v\geq 0\) and \(v\leq 2-u\text{,}\) we get a triangle with corresponding bounds \(0\leq u\leq 2\) and \(0\leq v\leq 2-u\text{,}\) or equivalently \(0\leq v\leq 2\) and \(0\leq u\leq 2-v\)

  2. We have

    \begin{align*} \vec{N} \amp = \vec{r}_{u} \times \vec{r}_{v} \\ \amp = \left( \vec{i} -\vec{k} \right) \times \left( \vec{j} -\vec{k} \right) \\ \amp = \vec{i} +\vec{j} +\vec{k}. \end{align*}

  3. We know \(\vec{N} =\vec{i} +\vec{j} +\vec{k}\) which has a positive \(\vec{k}\) component so points upward everywhere. It follows that \(\vec{r}\) is orientation preserving.
