Let \(\vec{u} =a\vec{i} +b\vec{j} +c\vec{k}\text{.}\) Calculating the dot product \(\vec{u} \cdot \vec{k} =c\text{,}\) condition (a) implies that \(c=2\text{.}\) Calculating the cross product \(\vec{u} \times \vec{k} =b\vec{i}-a\vec{j}\) implies that \(b=3\) and \(a=-2\text{.}\) Therefore, \(\vec{u} =-2\vec{i} +3\vec{j} +2\vec{k}\text{.}\)
