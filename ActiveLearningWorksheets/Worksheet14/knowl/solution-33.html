Solution 3.6.1.

The the upper half of the circle of radius \(2\) from \((2,0)\) to \((-2,0)\) can be parametrized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(t)=(2\cos t) \vec{i}+(2\sin t)\vec{j},\quad 0\leq t\leq \pi. \end{equation*}

Then we have \(\vec{r}'(t)=-2\sin{(t)} \vec{i} +2\cos{(t)} \vec{j}\) and so

\begin{align*} \int_{C} y ds \amp = \int_0^{\pi} \left(2\sin t \right) \sqrt{(-2\sin t)^2+(2\cos t)^2}dt\\ \amp = \int_0^{\pi} \left(2\sin t \right) \sqrt{4}dt\\ \amp = 4\left( -\cos t \right)_{0}^{\pi} \\ \amp = 8. \end{align*}
