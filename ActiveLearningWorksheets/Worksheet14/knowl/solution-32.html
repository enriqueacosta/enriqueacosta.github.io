Solution 3.5.1.

The curve \(y=x^2\) from from \((0,0)\) to \((3,9)\) can be parametrized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(t)=t\vec{i}+t^2\vec{j},\quad 0\leq t\leq 3. \end{equation*}

Then we have \(\vec{r}'(t)=\vec{i} +(2t)\vec{k}\) and so

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \int_{C} 3x ~ ds \amp = \int_0^{3} \left(3t \right) \sqrt{(1)^2+(2t)^2}~dt \\ \amp = \int_0^{3} 3t \sqrt{1+4t^2}~dt\\ \amp = \left( \frac{1}{4}\left(1+4t^2\right)^{\frac{3}{2}} \right)_{0}^{3}\\ \amp = \frac{1}{4}\left( \left(37\right)^{\frac{3}{2}} -1\right). \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
