Solution 3.4.1.

The line segment from \((3,4,0)\) to \((1,4,2)\) can be parametrized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(t)=(-2t+3)\vec{i}+4\vec{j} +(2t)\vec{k},\quad 0\leq t\leq 1. \end{equation*}

Then we have \(\vec{r}'(t)=-2\vec{i} +2\vec{k}\) and so

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \int_{C} (z+y^2) ~ ds \amp = \int_0^{1} \left((2t)+(4)^2\right) \sqrt{(-2)^2+(0)^2+(2)^2} ~ dt \\ \amp = \int_0^{1} (2t+16) \sqrt{8} ~ dt\\ \amp =\sqrt{8} \left( t^2 +16t\right)_{0}^{1}\\ \amp =17\sqrt{8}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
