Solution 3.3.1.

The line segment from \((1,3)\) to \((5,-2)\) can be parametrized by \[\vec{r}(t)=(4t+1)\vec{i}+(-5t+3)\vec{j},\quad 0\leq t\leq 1.\] Then we have \(\vec{r}'(t)=4\vec{i} -5\vec{j}\) and so

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \displaystyle\int_{C} (x-y) ds \amp = \int_0^{1} \left( (4t+1)-(-5t+3)\right) \sqrt{(4)^2+(-5)^2} dt \\ \amp = \int_0^{1} (9t-2) \sqrt{41} dt\\ \amp = \sqrt{41} \left( \frac{9t^2}{2} -2t\right)_{0}^{1}\\ \amp = \frac{5\sqrt{41}}{2}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
