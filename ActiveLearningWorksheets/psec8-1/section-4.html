Section 4 Second Set — Using Trig functions to find missing sides in right triangles

The simplest application of trig functions is finding a missing side when you know some sides and one of the angles.

All you have to do is figure out which one is the opposite and which one is the adjacent side and use the definitions!

Subsection 4.2 Wrap-Up

If you can answer the following questions, then you have a solid understanding of everything that was involved in this set!

  1. Describe in your own words how one finds an unknown length in a right triangle if one knowns an angle and one side. How does the side or angle you know affect what you should do?
  2. Assume you know no angles besides the right angle, but know all the sides. Can you use the trigonometric functions to find the angles? How?

If you are having trouble with one of the above, go over the solutions or ask your teacher.
