Inverse Trigonometric Functions.

If \(\theta\) is an angle in a right triangle (other than the right angle):

  • \(\theta = \cos^{-1}x\) is the angle \(\theta\) whose cosine is \(x\text{.}\)
  • \(\theta = \sin^{-1}x\) is the angle \(\theta\) whose sine is \(x\text{.}\)
  • \(\theta = \tan^{-1}x\) is the angle \(\theta\) whose tangent is \(x\text{.}\)
