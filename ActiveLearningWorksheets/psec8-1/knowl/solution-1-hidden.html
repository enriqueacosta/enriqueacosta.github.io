By the Pythagorean theorem, the hypotenuse has length \(\sqrt {10^2+5^2}\)=\(\sqrt{125} \text{.}\)

  1. \(\sin \theta = \displaystyle\frac{5}{\sqrt{125}}\)

  2. \(\sin \phi =\displaystyle\frac{10}{\sqrt{125}}\)

  3. \(\cos \theta = \displaystyle\frac{10}{\sqrt{125}}\)

  4. \(\cos \phi = \displaystyle\frac{5}{\sqrt{125}}\)

  5. \(\tan \theta = 5/10 = 1/2\)

  6. \(\tan \phi = 10/5 = 2\)
