Item 6.3.a.

Since the grade of the ramp is \({7} \% = {7}/100 \text{,}\) this means that a \({7}\)-foot height difference occurs over a horizontal distance of 100 feet. So we have \(\tan \theta = \frac{{7}}{100}.\) Using the \(\tan^{-1}\) button on the calculator, we get

\begin{equation*} \theta = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{{7}}{100}\right) = 4.00417 ^\circ. \end{equation*}
