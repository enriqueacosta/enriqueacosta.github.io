We have a right triangle with legs \(x\) and \(200\) and hypotenuse \(h\text{.}\) Thus,

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} \sin30^{\circ} \amp=\frac{200}{h}\\ h \amp= \frac{200}{\sin30^{\circ}} = \frac{200}{0.5} =400\,\text{feet.} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

To find the distance \(x\text{,}\) we can relate the angle and its opposite and adjacent legs by writing

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} \tan30^{\circ} \amp= \frac{200}{x}\\ x \amp= \frac{200}{\tan30^{\circ}}\approx346.410\,\text{feet.} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

We could also write the equation \(x^2+200^2=h^2\) and substitute \(h=400\)ft to solve for \(x\text{.}\)
