Section 5 Third Set: What if the angles are not known?

Even if we don't know any of the angles (besides the right angle), we can still use the Pythagorean theorem together with trig functions and their inverses to figure out all sides and angles in a right triangle.

Pythagorean Theorem.

In a right triangle with hypotenuse \(c\) and sides \(a\) and \(b\) we have

\begin{equation*} a^2+b^2=c^2 \end{equation*}
