Section 2 Summary of Section — Your Toolbox

Definition of trigonometric functions using right triangles.

For an angle \(\theta\) in a right triangle (not the right angle):

\begin{equation*} \sin\theta=\frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}},\qquad \cos\theta=\frac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Hypotenuse}},\qquad \tan\theta=\frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Adjacent}}. \end{equation*}
Inverse Trigonometric Functions.

If \(\theta\) is an angle in a right triangle (other than the right angle):

  • \(\theta = \cos^{-1}x\) is the angle \(\theta\) whose cosine is \(x\text{.}\)
  • \(\theta = \sin^{-1}x\) is the angle \(\theta\) whose sine is \(x\text{.}\)
  • \(\theta = \tan^{-1}x\) is the angle \(\theta\) whose tangent is \(x\text{.}\)
Trigonometric Functions of Special Angles.
\begin{align*} \cos\left(0^\circ \right) \amp=1\amp \qquad \sin\left(0^\circ \right) \amp=0 \amp \qquad \tan\left(0^\circ \right) \amp=0\\ \cos\left(30^\circ \right) \amp=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\amp \qquad \sin\left(30^\circ \right) \amp=\frac{1}{2} \amp \qquad \tan\left(30^\circ \right) \amp=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\\ \cos\left(45^\circ \right) \amp=\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \amp \qquad \sin\left(45^\circ \right) \amp=\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \amp \qquad \tan\left(45^\circ \right) \amp=1\\ \cos\left(60^\circ \right) \amp=\frac{1}{2} \amp \qquad \sin\left(60^\circ \right) \amp =\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \amp \qquad \tan\left(60^\circ \right) \amp=\sqrt{3}\\ \cos\left(90^\circ \right) \amp =0\amp \qquad \sin\left(90^\circ \right) \amp =1 \amp \qquad \tan\left(90^\circ \right) \amp \text{ is undefined} \end{align*}
