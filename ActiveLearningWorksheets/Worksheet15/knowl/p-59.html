The equation \(\vec{r}(t) =\cos t \vec{i} +\sin t \vec{j}\) gives a circle of radius \(1\) in the \(xy\)-plane oriented counter-clockwise when viewed from above and centered at \((0,0)\) starting (when \(t=0\)) at \((1,0)\text{.}\) To get a circle of radius \(5\) with starting point \((5,0)\text{,}\) we scale each component by \(5\text{,}\) and to make the curve spiral around the \(z\)-axis, we add the component \(t\vec{k}\text{.}\) Thus \(\vec{r}(t)=5\cos(t)\vec{i} + 5\sin(t)\vec{j}+t\vec{k}\) is a vector equation for the described curve.
