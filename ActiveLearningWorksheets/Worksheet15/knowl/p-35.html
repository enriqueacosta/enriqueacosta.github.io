The equation \(\vec{r}(t) =\cos t \vec{i} +\sin t \vec{j}\text{,}\) \(0\leq t\leq 2\pi\) gives a full circle of radius \(1\) oriented counter-clockwise starting at \((1,0)\) when \(t=0\text{.}\) To get a circle of radius \(3\text{,}\) we scale each component by \(3\text{.}\) Thus \(\vec{r}(t) =3\cos t \vec{i} +3\sin t \vec{j}\) for \(0\leq t\leq 2\pi\) is a circle of radius \(3\) centered at \((0,0)\) oriented counterclockwise starting at \((0,3)\) when \(t=0\text{.}\) To get the quarter circle, we restrict \(0\leq t\leq \pi/2\text{.}\)
