Solution 2.4.1.

The equation \(\vec{r}(t) =\cos t \vec{i} +\sin t \vec{k}\text{,}\) \(0\leq t\leq 2\pi\) gives a full circle oriented counter-clockwise starting (when \(t=0\)) at \((1,0,0)\) in the \(xz\)-plane. To get a circle of radius \(2\text{,}\) we scale each component by \(2\text{,}\) and to move the center to \((1,4,1)\) we add the vector \(\vec{i} +4\vec{j}+\vec{k}\) (which translates every point in the \(x\)-direction by \(1\text{,}\) the \(y\)-direction by \(4\) and the \(z\)-direction by \(1\)). Thus

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(t) =(1+2\cos t)\vec{i} +4\vec{j}+(1+ 2\sin(t))\vec{k} \end{equation*}

for \(0\leq t\leq 2\pi\) is a circle of radius \(2\) centered at \((1,4,1)\) in the plane \(y=4\) starting at the point \((3,2,1)\text{.}\) To get the half circle, we restrict \(0\leq t\leq \pi\text{.}\)
